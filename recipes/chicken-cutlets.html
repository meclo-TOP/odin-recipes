Chicken Cutlets

Description

Golden, crunchy, and impossibly juicy. These crispy chicken cutlets boast a shattering crust that gives way to tender, savory inside. Pan-fried to perfection and ready in minutes, they're the ultimate comfort food upgrade. Better than takeout, easy to make, and guaranteed to disappear fast.

Ingredients

  • Chicken Breasts
  • Flour
  • Egg
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Seasonings (salt, pepper, optional additional seasoning)
  • Parmesan Cheese
  • Oil (extra virgin olive oil preferred)

Directions

  1. Prepare the Chicken
    • "Butterfly" the chicken breasts (cut in half lengthwise). This is why they are called Cutlets!
    • Place the halved breasts in between two sheets of parchment paper or plastic wrap. Then use a meat mallet to thin out the chicken, work from the center to the edges
    • Season chicken with salt and pepper, additonal seasonings recommended to add in the next step

  2. Prepare the dredging station
    • Set up three bowls/trays
    • In one add flour, in another add eggs, and lastly add the breadcrumbs + seasonings. salt pepper and parmesan are reccommended but feel free to experiment with different flavors

  3. Coat the Chicken in the following order: Flour, Egg, Breadcrumbs
    • Reserve one hand for working with wet ingredients and the other for dry
    • Allow excess egg to drip off before dropping into breadcrumbs
    • Firmly press breadcrumbs into cutlet using your knuckles

  4. Cook the cutlets
    • Heat your pan, then add enough oil to cover half of your prepped cutlets (about one inch)
    • Wait for oil to get hot , test by dropping in a breadcumb , should immediately sizzle
    • Add cutlets, cook until golden brown (about 3-5 minutes per side depending on the temperature of oil)
    • Place finished cutlest on a wire rack lined tray to allow for oil drip
    • Over time oil will evaporate or be absorbed by the cutlets, so keep an eye on your oil level and add more accordingly
